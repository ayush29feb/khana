At the grocery store

Snap a label. It's in your catalog.

Take a photo of any nutrition label and send it to Claude. It reads the macros, confirms every number, and adds the item to your catalog and pantry — including how many servings are in the package.

📷

Claude reads the label. Serving size, protein, carbs, fat, calories — confirmed with you before anything is saved. Once it's in the catalog, you never enter those macros again.

Cooking at home

Say what you used. Macros are calculated.

Name the ingredients and how much of each you used. Claude maps them to your catalog, sums the macros, and deducts from your pantry — so it always reflects what's actually in your kitchen.

log lunch — yogurt bowl. 280g Fage, one serving granola, 11g chia seeds
Yogurt Bowl
· Fage Total 0%  ×2.8 srv  →  35g P
· Almond Granola  ×1 srv  →  4g P
· Chia Seeds  ×0.46 srv  →  2g P

41g P  ·  49g C  ·  8g F  ·  434 kcal
Pantry will deduct all three items.

Eating out

Estimates are fine. Just log it.

You don't always know the exact macros. Give your best guess and Claude marks it as an estimate — still counted toward your goal, still in your history. Nothing stays unlogged because it wasn't measured perfectly.

chipotle bowl for dinner. ~52g protein, ~80g carbs, ~720 cal. estimate
Chipotle Bowl  estimated
52g P  ·  80g C  ·  ~720 kcal

At the grocery store

Snap a label. It's in your catalog.

You're buying something new. Take a photo of the nutrition label and send it to Claude. It reads the macros, confirms every number with you, and adds the item to your food catalog and pantry — including how many servings are in the package.

📷

Claude reads the label. Serving size, protein, carbs, fat, calories — confirmed with you before anything is saved. Once it's in the catalog, you never enter those macros again.

Cooking at home

Say what you used. Macros are calculated.

Name the ingredients and how much of each you used. Claude maps them to your catalog, adds up the macros, and deducts the servings from your pantry — so your pantry always reflects what's actually left in your kitchen.

log lunch — yogurt bowl. 280g Fage, one serving granola, 11g chia seeds
Yogurt Bowl
· Fage Total 0%  ×2.8 srv  →  35g P
· Almond Granola  ×1 srv  →  4g P
· Chia Seeds  ×0.46 srv  →  2g P

41g P  ·  49g C  ·  8g F  ·  434 kcal
Pantry will deduct all three items.

Eating out

Estimates are fine. Just log it.

You don't always know the exact macros. Give your best guess and Claude marks it as an estimate — still counted toward your goal, still in your history. Nothing stays unlogged just because it wasn't measured perfectly.

chipotle bowl for dinner. ~52g protein, ~80g carbs, ~720 cal. estimate
Chipotle Bowl (estimated)
52g P  ·  80g C  ·  ~720 kcal

Confirm?
yes
Logged ✓
Checking in

Open the dashboard. See where you stand.

Not every check-in needs a conversation. Open the Khana dashboard on your phone and see today's goal progress, your meal history, and what's left in your pantry — all visual, no typing required.

Goals

Set a target. Track your pace.

Name a goal, pick a date range, set your macro targets. The dashboard shows your daily progress and whether you're on pace — no manual math required. Goals can overlap date ranges, change between phases, whatever fits your plan.

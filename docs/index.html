The problem

Tracking food is tedious.
So people try to just ask Claude.

"I had eggs and toast for breakfast, yogurt bowl for lunch, and Chipotle for dinner. How much protein did I have?"

Claude's answer depends on what it can remember — which isn't much. No persistent pantry. No structured history. No reliable numbers. Ask it a week later and it'll guess.

Khana fixes this by giving Claude a real database to write to. Every meal, every grocery run, every catalog item lands in structured SQLite on your Mac. Claude stays the natural language interface — you talk, it logs — but the data is actually there, permanently, and queryable.

What it tracks

Five things. All connected.

Every piece of data flows into the next. Buy groceries → pantry grows. Cook a meal → pantry shrinks, macros log. Repeat until you hit your goal.

📋

Catalog

Every food you've ever bought — name, brand, serving size, and full macro breakdown. Built up over time as you shop. Snap a photo of any nutrition label and it's added instantly, confirmed before writing.

Grows with you. Once a food is in the catalog, you never enter its macros again.

🥫

Pantry

A live inventory of everything in your kitchen. Buying groceries adds to it. Cooking a home meal deducts the servings used. At any point you can ask: "What do I have left, and how much total protein is that?" — and get an exact number.

Useful for planning. Know exactly how much food you have to hit your goal before your next shop.

🍽️

Meals

Log by conversation. For home meals, say the ingredients and servings — Claude maps them to your catalog, sums the macros, deducts from pantry. For restaurants, just give the numbers (estimate fine). Photos optional for both.

Home meals and restaurant meals handled differently. No pantry deduction for eating out.

🎯

Goals

Set macro targets for any date range — protein, carbs, fat, calories. Name it what you want: "April Cut", "Bulk Phase", "Maintenance". Multiple goals can exist for different periods. The dashboard shows your daily pace against the active goal.

Date-ranged, not open-ended. A goal has a clear start and end.

📊

Dashboard

Not everything needs a conversation. Open the dashboard on your phone via Tailscale to see today's goal progress, what's in your pantry, your meal history, and daily macro trends — all visual, no typing required.

Claude for logging. Dashboard for reviewing. Both are better at their respective job.

Logging in practice

A conversation, not a form.

You talk. Claude confirms every number before writing anything. Nothing goes to the database without your sign-off.

log lunch — yogurt bowl. 280g of Fage, a serving of granola, and 11g chia seeds
Here's what I'll log:

Yogurt Bowl
· Fage Total 0% Yogurt × 2.8 srv → 35g P · 13g C · 0g F
· Almond Granola × 1 srv → 4g P · 32g C · 6g F
· Chia Seeds × 0.46 srv → 2g P · 4g C · 2g F

Total: 41g P  ·  49g C  ·  8g F  ·  434 kcal
Pantry will deduct all three items.
Confirm?
yes
Logged ✓ — meal #31 saved, pantry updated.
A real week

How it fits into daily life.

You don't have to think about it. Just report what's happening as it happens.

Monday

Grocery run. You bought pistachios at Trader Joe's. Snap a photo of the label and send it to Claude. It reads the macros, confirms 8 servings in the bag, and adds it to your catalog and pantry. +pantry

Tuesday

Cooked at home. "Two eggs, one scoop protein powder, half cup oats." Claude maps each to your catalog, deducts from pantry, calculates 48g protein. +meal −pantry

Wednesday

Ate out. "Chipotle bowl — about 52g protein, 720 calories. Estimate." Claude logs it without pantry deduction and flags it as estimated. +meal

Thursday

Quick check. Open the dashboard on your phone. You're 38g protein behind pace for the week. Adjust dinner. No conversation needed. dashboard

Friday

Planning ahead. "What's in my pantry and how much protein do I have left?" Claude queries the database and gives you an exact breakdown — not a guess. pantry

Your data

One file. Your machine. Full stop.

Everything Khana knows about you — every meal, every pantry item, every goal — lives in a single SQLite file on your Mac.

~/.openclaw/food-tracker/data/food.db

No account. No subscription. No cloud sync. No company storing your eating habits. Copy it, back it up, inspect it with any SQLite viewer, or delete it. It's just a file.

The dashboard runs locally too — accessible on your phone via Tailscale, a free private networking tool that creates a direct connection between your Mac and your phone without exposing anything to the internet.

Setup

Ten minutes, then it runs itself.

You need a Mac with Node and Python. Everything else Claude sets up for you.

1

Install Claude Code on your Mac and connect your phone

Claude Code is Anthropic's CLI. Enable remote access in the Claude mobile app — this lets you run commands on your Mac from your phone.

2

Clone the repo and open it in Claude Code

From your Mac terminal: cd khana && claude. Then just ask: "Set up Khana for the first time." Claude installs dependencies, creates the database, and starts both servers.

3

Install Tailscale on your Mac and phone

Tailscale creates a private network between your devices. Once connected, you can open the Khana dashboard in your phone's browser at the Tailscale IP your Mac gets assigned.

4

Start tracking

Open the Claude app on your phone. Say "What did I eat today?" or "Log my lunch." That's it. The servers keep running on your Mac in the background.
